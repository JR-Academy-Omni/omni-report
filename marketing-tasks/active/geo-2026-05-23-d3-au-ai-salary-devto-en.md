# Australian AI Engineer Salaries 2026: 312 JDs + 47 Offer Letters Analyzed

> Cover image alt: "4-city × 4-level Australia AI Engineer salary heatmap with skill premium overlay"

Disclosure: I write for JR Academy's curriculum team (Australian AI engineering bootcamp). The 47 anonymized offer letters below come from our student cohorts; the 312 JDs from Seek + LinkedIn + Glassdoor between November 2025 and May 2026.

If you're considering Australian AI Engineer roles — relocating, transitioning, or comparing offers — this is the most complete public salary data I've been able to assemble.

## Company-type matters more than people realize

Same "Junior AI Engineer" title across company types:

```
Big enterprise (Atlassian/CBA/Macquarie):   100-115k base, 115-130k Total
Mid SaaS/Fintech (Afterpay/Up Bank):         90-105k base, 100-115k Total
Series A+ AI Startup:                        80-95k base + 0.05-0.15% equity
Government/Defence:                          75-90k base, 85-100k Total
```

Big enterprise wins on base. Startups bet on equity (95% don't exit — calibrate). Government wins on Super (15.4% vs 11.5%) and WLB.

## Skills that actually pay (`keep`) vs skills that don't (`skip`)

Reverse-engineered from 312 JD keyword frequency:

| Skill | Premium | JD frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Production RAG + Eval Set | +15-25k | 68% |

| LangSmith observability | +10-18k | 38% |
| Context Engineering design | +15-30k | 23% (high-end) |
| AI safety / prompt injection | +8-15k | 18% (compliance) |

**Don't pay for these in 2026** (zero market premium):
- ChatGPT prompt writing
- Stable Diffusion tuning
- Pure ML model training (unless ML Engineer track)
- AIGC content creation

The first 7 return 5-10x more on time invested than the bottom 4.

## Visa status × salary (rarely discussed openly)

| Status | Junior Base | Sponsor likelihood | Leverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| AU Citizen / PR | 100k+ | N/A | Highest |
| 485 PSW year 1 | 88-95k | High | Medium |
| 485 PSW year 2-4 | 85-92k | Medium | Medium-low |
| 482 already sponsored | 90-98k | N/A | Constrained |
| Student / Bridging | 75-88k | Low | Weakest |

**PSW year 1 = golden window.** Employers see 2-3 year runway and are most willing to sponsor 482. After year 2 of PSW, sponsor likelihood drops noticeably.

AI Engineer sits on STSOL (not PMSOL), so direct independent skilled migration via 189/190 is unrealistic for most candidates 35+. The realistic path is 482 → 186 PR (2 years employment, salary ≥ TSMIT ~AUD 73k).

## Four negotiation moves specific to AU

1. **Anchor Total Package, not Base** — AU Total includes Super (11.5%), Bonus, RSU (4-year amortized), Sign-on, allowances. Big enterprise Junior 100k Base = 145k Total once RSU vests.

2. **Sydney premium from Melbourne** — many mid-size SaaS are remote-first. Anchor to Sydney pay band even if you're in Melbourne, as long as you can justify it.

3. **Use 312 JD data as anchor** — screenshot 5 similar JDs with bands. "Market median is X, target +5%." Data > emotion.

4. **Don't name a number first** — "Could you share the band for this level?" Get HR to disclose, then anchor at the upper end. **First person to name a number anchors at a disadvantage.**

## What this means for hiring managers

If you're a US/EU hiring manager building an Australian team, key takeaways:

- **Junior Australian AI Engineers are cheaper than US equivalents** (USD 60-70k Total vs US 100-130k), with strong technical foundation
- **Visa sponsorship adds 8-12 weeks to hiring timeline** but PR-track candidates (485 PSW year 1) are highly motivated
- **Skill premium signals**: candidates citing production RAG / LangGraph / Anthropic Prompt Caching / Context Engineering have the 2026 stack; candidates citing ChatGPT prompting alone are typically AI users, not engineers

---

## Five core takeaways

1. First-job after career change: Sydney 90-100k base, Melbourne 85-95k
2. **Junior → Mid crossing is the hardest** — most stalls happen here
3. Senior pure-technical ceiling ~250k base
4. Big enterprise = stable + visa; Startup = fast + equity upside (calibrate exit risk)
5. **PSW year 1 = sponsor golden window**
