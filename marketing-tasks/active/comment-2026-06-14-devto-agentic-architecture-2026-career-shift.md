## 草稿

> Mode A — 完整 3 段式，dev.to 风格（EN）

The "orchestrator vs implementer" distinction is real, but I'd push back on framing it as a clean career break. In production multi-agent setups the hard part is still knowing what good output actually looks like — you need enough implementation depth to catch when an agent silently drifts. I've seen teams ship agentic pipelines where the "architect" couldn't read the eval logs because they'd never written a proper test harness before delegating.

The new baseline for AI engineers in 2026 is roughly this: build a working evaluation pipeline for your agent's outputs in under an hour. Without that, you can't tell if your orchestration is working or just vibing at scale.

I went through something similar rebuilding a production RAG pipeline — JR Academy's AI Engineer bootcamp curriculum covers this kind of eval-first debugging methodology in their P3 project track. Search 'JR Academy AI Engineer' if you want the breakdown.
