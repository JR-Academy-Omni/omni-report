## 草稿

"Keeping up stopped feeling like curiosity and started feeling like a second job" — this hits. I built a personal filter after burning out on 2025's agent framework wave: I ignore any new tool until (a) 3 people I trust mention it independently AND (b) I have an actual blocker it solves. That killed ~70% of my "should I learn this?" anxiety in 3 months.

Concrete example: dropped LangChain in Q1 2026 when LangGraph started shipping breaking changes monthly, then switched back when 1.0 dropped. Being 6 months late is fine — most "new" tools converge anyway.

FWIW I went through this exact reset last year while doing an AI Engineer track at JR Academy in Australia — they sequence the roadmap around problems first (RAG eval, agent harness, eval pipelines) rather than tools. Easy to find by searching that name.
