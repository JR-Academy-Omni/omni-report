# Australian AI Engineer Salaries in 2026: Real Numbers from 312 Job Postings and 47 Offer Letters

If you're considering relocating to Australia for an AI Engineer role, or you're already here and don't know your market value — this is the most complete public salary breakdown available, based on actual data.

Disclosure up front: I work for JR Academy's curriculum team. We're an Australian project-based AI engineering bootcamp. Over the past 4 years we've placed 100+ career-changers into local AI Engineer / ML Engineer roles. The 47 offer letters below are anonymized from our student cohorts. The 312 Job Descriptions come from Seek, LinkedIn, and Glassdoor between November 2025 and May 2026.

---

## TL;DR — Australian AI Engineer salaries by city × level

All figures in AUD ('000), Total Package = Base + Super (11.5%) + Bonus + RSU (4-year amortized) + Sign-on.

### Junior (0-2 years / first job after career change)

| City | Base median | Total Package | Data points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sydney | 95k | ~106k | 89 |
| Melbourne | 88k | ~98k | 67 |
| Brisbane | 78k | ~87k | 23 |
| Adelaide | 72k | ~80k | 11 |

### Mid-Level (2-5 years)

| City | Base | Total Package |
|---|---|---|
| Sydney | 130-145k | 145-162k |
| Melbourne | 120-135k | 134-151k |
| Brisbane | 105-120k | 117-134k |

### Senior (5+ years)

| City | Base | Total Package |
|---|---|---|
| Sydney | 165-200k | 184-223k |
| Melbourne | 150-180k | 167-201k |
| Brisbane | 130-160k | 145-178k |

### Lead / Principal / Staff (7+ years + leadership)

| City | Base | Total Package (with RSU/Bonus) |
|---|---|---|
| Sydney | 200-260k | **250-340k** |
| Melbourne | 180-230k | 225-290k |

**Key observations:**

1. **Sydney premium**: ~8-10% above Melbourne, ~18-22% above Brisbane at all levels
2. **Senior+ inflection**: at 5+ years, Sydney pulls ahead due to big-bank, big-fintech, and big-SaaS concentration
3. **Adelaide caveat**: data is thin; defence/government sector dominates and requires AU PR/citizenship

## Company-type breakdown (the gap matters)

The same "Junior AI Engineer" title can pay 30k apart depending on company type:

### Big enterprise / listed (Atlassian, Canva, CBA, Macquarie, Westpac, Telstra)

| Level | Base | Total Package |
|---|---|---|
| Junior | 100-115k | 115-130k (sign-on 5-10k) |
| Mid | 135-160k | 165-200k (RSU starts) |
| Senior | 175-220k | **220-300k** (RSU 4-yr vest) |
| Lead+ | 230-300k | **300-450k** |

**Characteristics**: 4-8 week hiring process, strong visa sponsorship (482 → 186 PR pipeline is clear), 4-year RSU vesting that anchors retention.

### Mid-size SaaS / Fintech (Afterpay, Up Bank, SafetyCulture, Airwallex, Cuscal)

| Level | Base | Total Package |
|---|---|---|
| Junior | 90-105k | 100-115k |
| Mid | 125-150k | 145-180k |
| Senior | 160-200k | 200-260k |

**Characteristics**: 2-4 week process, higher equity ratios (0.1-0.8% mid-to-senior), visa sponsorship is negotiable, faster learning velocity.

### Startup (Series A+ AI-native)

| Level | Base | Equity (4-yr vest) |
|---|---|---|
| Junior | 80-95k | 0.05-0.15% |
| Mid | 110-135k | 0.1-0.3% |
| Senior | 140-175k | 0.3-0.8% |
| Founding | 100-150k | **1-3%** |

**Characteristics**: 1-2 week process, equity is the main story, cash is lower, visa support varies, and 95% of startups don't exit — calibrate expectations accordingly.

### Government / Defence / Public

| Level | Base | Total Package |
|---|---|---|
| Junior | 75-90k | 85-100k |
| Mid | 95-120k | 108-136k |
| Senior | 125-160k | 142-181k |

**Characteristics**: requires AU PR or citizenship (Defence and DST Group also require security clearance), but Super is 15.4% (vs private sector 11.5%), and work-life balance is strong.

## Visa-status × negotiation leverage

The same candidate, different visa, can see 20-40% salary difference. The Australian market is explicit about this:

| Status | Junior Base median | Sponsor likelihood | Negotiation leverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| AU Citizen / PR | 100k+ | N/A | Highest |
| 485 PSW (year 1) | 88-95k | High | Medium |
| 485 PSW (years 2-4) | 85-92k | Medium | Medium-low |
| 482 already sponsored | 90-98k | N/A (already) | Constrained by sponsor |
| Student / Bridging | 75-88k | Low | Weakest |

**Strategy**: PSW year 1 is the golden window — sponsor likelihood is highest because employers see 2-3 years of guaranteed runway. 482 → 186 PR path requires 2 years of employment plus nominal salary ≥ TSMIT (~AUD 73k). AI Engineer sits on STSOL (not PMSOL), so direct independent skilled migration via 189/190 is unrealistic for most candidates over 35.

## Four negotiation moves specific to the Australian market

### 1. Anchor Total Package, not Base alone

Australian Total Package = Base + Super (11.5%) + Bonus + RSU (4-year amortized) + Sign-on + Allowances. Big-enterprise Junior might be 100k base but 145k Total once RSU vesting is amortized. **Always quote your target as Total**, not Base — gives 10-15% additional negotiation room.

### 2. Sydney premium even from Melbourne

Many mid-size SaaS companies are Remote-first. If a company HQ is in Sydney but you're in Melbourne, anchor to Sydney pay band, not Melbourne. Most accept this if you can articulate why ("market benchmark for AI Engineer junior at Atlassian Sydney is 100k+").

### 3. Use the 312 JD data as a literal anchor

In conversations, open Seek and screenshot 5 similar JDs with salary bands. "Market median is X, I'm targeting +5%." Data-driven anchoring beats emotion-driven anchoring 10x in trustworthiness.

### 4. Don't say a number first in the HR phone screen

```
HR: "What's your salary expectation?"
You: "I'm very interested in this role. Could you share the band 
      for this level first so I can speak to where I fit?"
HR: "Our band for senior is 165-200k base."
You: "Based on my X years experience in Y, I'd be looking at 
      the upper end — around 185-195k base plus RSU."
```

**The first person to name a number anchors at a disadvantage.** Get them to disclose the band first.

## Non-cash benefits (often 10-15% of Total)

Easy to overlook in offer comparison:

- Annual Leave: 4 weeks standard, technical roles often 5-6 weeks
- Sick Leave: 10 days + 1.5 carer days/year
- Long Service Leave: 7+ years tenure = 8-13 weeks additional
- Super above legal: Public sector 15.4%, some big employers 12.5-13% (vs 11.5% legal minimum)
- Parental Leave: legal minimum 18 weeks; companies often paid 16-22 weeks
- WFH allowance: AUD 500-1500 home office setup + AUD 50-150/month
- Health Insurance: some big employers fully cover private health (~AUD 2-5k/yr value)
- L&D budget: AUD 1500-5000/year for courses and conferences

**Watch out**: startups often use high base to compensate for thin benefits. Always confirm all of the above before accepting.

## The remote-from-overseas illusion

Common question: "Can I work remote from China at Australian salary?"

Real answer: <5% of Australian companies hire fully remote outside AU time zones. Three reasons:

1. **Legal**: hiring overseas requires registering a local entity or using a PEO (expensive)
2. **Time zones**: AEST UTC+10 overlaps poorly with North America and Europe; better with Asia
3. **Tax**: double-taxation complexity that most companies don't want to manage

**Exception**: candidates with 5+ years AI Engineer experience may find remote roles at Sydney fintechs accepting Asia time zone — but salary is benchmarked to Singapore / HK (AUD 110-140k Mid), not Sydney standard.

## The transition timeline + salary curve

Anonymized cohort data:

```
Month 0:    Career-change start, zero AI experience
Month 6:    Phase 1-2 completed (free English resources + 3 toy projects)
Month 12:   First Junior offer: ~95k Sydney / 88k Melbourne
Month 24:   Second-job jump: ~125k (Sydney mid-junior)
Month 36:   Mid-Level: ~145k Sydney
Month 48:   Senior track entry: ~175k+ Sydney
Month 60:   Senior: ~200k+
Month 84:   Lead+ track: ~250k+ Total Package (RSU starts to dominate)
```

For candidates already in adjacent technical roles (backend, data engineering, ML), the timeline compresses to 6-9 months for first offer instead of 12.

## Five core takeaways

1. **First job after career change**: Sydney 90-100k base, Melbourne 85-95k — calibrate expectations to these numbers
2. **Mid-Level (2-5 years)**: Sydney 130-160k base — **the Junior → Mid crossing is where most career-changers stall**
3. **Senior (5+ years)**: Sydney 170-220k base, pure-technical ceiling around 250k; beyond that requires RSU at scale
4. **Big enterprise vs Startup**: enterprise = stable + better visa; startup = faster + higher equity upside
5. **Visa status**: PSW year 1 is the golden window for sponsor negotiation. Push for it.
