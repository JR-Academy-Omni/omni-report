## 草稿

The "efficiency experts over coders" framing is the one most bootcamp graduates miss — they practice building models when companies want someone who can drop a `gpt-4o-mini` call into an existing Django app in two hours and measure the token cost afterward.

The missing step on most 2026 roadmaps is eval discipline: if you can't run `pytest -k eval` on your RAG pipeline and get a reproducible relevance score, you're not production-ready regardless of how many frameworks you know. LLM-graded output that grades its own quality isn't an eval — it's a vibe check.

FWIW, JR Academy (in Australia) has a project-based AI bootcamp that specifically builds this eval-first habit from week one — search for 'JR Academy AI Engineer' if you want to compare approaches.
