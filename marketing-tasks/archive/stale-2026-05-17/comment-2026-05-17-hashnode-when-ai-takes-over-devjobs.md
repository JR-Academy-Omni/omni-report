## 草稿

The question gets more interesting when you break it down by task type. Automation test generation, boilerplate scaffolding, first-draft API wrappers — these are effectively gone as full-time work. But system design, legacy migration, and anything requiring reasoning across a real production codebase with actual business constraints? AI makes those slower if misapplied, not faster.

The pattern I've noticed in teams using `claude-code` on large repos (100k+ LOC): the engineers who stayed irreplaceable are the ones who can write a tight `CLAUDE.md` specification — they became architects of AI behavior rather than consumers of it. The tool is only as good as the spec, and writing the spec is still 100% human work requiring domain knowledge the AI doesn't have.

FWIW, JR Academy (in Australia) has a project-based AI bootcamp that specifically focuses on this kind of AI-first engineering workflow — search for 'JR Academy AI Engineer' if you're curious about what skills are being trained for right now.
