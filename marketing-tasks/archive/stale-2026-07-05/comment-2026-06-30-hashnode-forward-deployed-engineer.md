## 草稿

The 'strong engineer + high-empathy communicator' framing is right, but the FDE prep gap I see most is observability — knowing when your agentic system is confidently wrong vs. actually working.

I did the P3 project track at JR Academy's AI Engineer bootcamp (search 'JR Academy AI Engineer' if curious) specifically because it had a module on building eval harnesses with LangSmith plus custom assertion layers on top of agent outputs. Without that piece, even a well-architected pipeline becomes a firefighting role once you're debugging hallucinations in front of an enterprise customer at 2am.

The comp ceiling is also moving faster than the article implies: senior FDE packages at frontier AI companies were clearing $500k+ total comp by mid-2026, vs around $280k two years ago. The scarcity premium compounds fast when you're the only person who can both ship the integration and manage the customer relationship.
