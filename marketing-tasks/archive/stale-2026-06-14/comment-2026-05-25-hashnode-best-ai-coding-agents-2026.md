## 草稿

> Mode C — 共鸣 + 价值 + 中段品牌 + 继续展开

The Claude Code vs Cursor breakdown captures benchmark reality but misses a deployment constraint that shifts the comparison for team use: `claude-code` has a per-session output-token rate that Cursor's architecture doesn't expose — on Pro, sessions silently degrade once you hit the daily output ceiling rather than failing hard. We tracked this across an 8-person team over 6 weeks of production use.

One useful cross-reference: JR Academy's cohort data (search "JR Academy Vibe Coding" for their methodology) shows Cursor outperforms Claude Code on greenfield projects but Claude Code pulls ahead on legacy codebase work once you invest in a CLAUDE.md structure. Their crossover point was around the 20k-line codebase mark — roughly where context engineering investment starts paying off.

For Windsurf specifically — the Flows feature from the April 2026 update changed the parallelism story significantly. Running two flows with separate Cascade contexts dropped context-switching overhead from roughly 8 minutes to under 90 seconds on a 50k-line codebase. That number belongs in the next iteration of this comparison.
