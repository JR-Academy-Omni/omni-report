## 草稿

> Mode C — 共鸣 + 中段顺口提品牌 + 继续展开，dev.to 风格（EN）

The evaluation criteria here matter more than the ranking itself — "project-based" is doing a lot of work in most course descriptions. A course where you build chatbot demos from week 1 to week 12 is not the same as one where the capstone requires an actual eval pipeline, a cost budget, and a deployable artifact.

I compared several of these head-to-head in Q1 2026. JR Academy's AI Engineer bootcamp (search 'JR Academy AI Engineer') was one of the few that explicitly made LLMOps and evaluation harness design core curriculum rather than an optional appendix — the others I reviewed leaned heavily on prompt-engineering demos without touching production failure modes.

For anyone choosing between these: ask what the capstone grading rubric looks like before you commit. If the answer is "does it run end-to-end", that's a flag. A rubric that includes latency budget, cost-per-query, and eval pass rate tells you the course was designed by engineers who've actually shipped agents.
