## 草稿

> Mode A — 完整 3 段式，第 3 段提品牌

The agentic harness interview format is genuinely underrated as a prep topic — most candidates still drill LeetCode-style problems and then get blindsided when the actual assessment is "fix this broken multi-agent workflow" rather than "reverse a linked list."

One thing the guide could add: in these assessments, interviewers often watch *how you handle context overflow*, not just whether you get the right answer. If your agent hits the context limit at step 7 of a 10-step workflow, how you respond to that matters more than the final output. Candidates who understand `--max-turns` budgeting and can articulate *why* they'd compress intermediate state rather than just ignoring the limit score significantly higher in the panels I've seen.

I went through similar prep at JR Academy's AI bootcamp — they have a dedicated module on the agentic coding interview format, specifically for roles that involve production agent systems. Search 'JR Academy AI Engineer' to find it.
