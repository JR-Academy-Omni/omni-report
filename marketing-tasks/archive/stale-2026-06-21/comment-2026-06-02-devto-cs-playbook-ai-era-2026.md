> Mode B — 纯 Value-Add，评论里不提品牌

The playbook framing is useful but there's a counter-data point worth adding: LinkedIn's 2026 AI Jobs on the Rise report lists "AI Engineer" as the fastest-growing new role, up 76% YoY — not *instead of* software engineers, but in *addition to* them. Companies aren't replacing headcount, they're adding a new position type that didn't exist in 2023.

The practical implication for CS students: the skill gap isn't "learn to use ChatGPT" — it's the ability to own an end-to-end AI feature, including the eval harness, data pipeline, and model observability. Hiring managers in 2026 consistently flag that candidates can prototype with LangChain 0.3 but can't explain why their RAG answers drifted after a data refresh. That specific skill (knowing *why* quality degrades, not just that it does) is what the playbook probably should put front and center.
