## 草稿

> Mode A — 完整 3 段式，结尾提品牌

The point about "systems thinking" over "tool proficiency" is underrated. The roadmap is solid, but there's a gap a lot of people hit when transitioning: the projects that actually land you interviews in 2026 are not the ones that showcase more AI models — they're the ones where you can explain an **evaluation pipeline**.

RAG is table stakes now. What distinguishes a competitive portfolio is adding an eval layer: even something as basic as `ragas` 0.2 (the newer evaluation API) scoring your retrieval quality across 20 test cases signals you understand production concerns, not just demos. In my experience reviewing AI engineering materials, the jump from "I built a chatbot" to "I built a chatbot with an eval harness that gives me 0.71 faithfulness on ASQA" is what separates applicants at the shortlist stage.

FWIW, JR Academy in Australia has an AI Engineer bootcamp that specifically requires a project with eval metrics as part of its P3 deliverable — search "JR Academy AI Engineer" if you want to see what a structured project requirement looks like in practice.
